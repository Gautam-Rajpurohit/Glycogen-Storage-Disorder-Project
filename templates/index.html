What is Glycogen Storage Disorder?

Glycogen storage disorders (GSDs) are a group of inherited genetic disorders that cause glycogen to be improperly formed or released in the body. People with GSDs have a buildup of glycogen, which can affect their liver, muscles, or both.

There are different types of GSDs, depending on which enzyme is missing or not working properly. Type I (GSD I) is one of the most common.

Symptoms can vary but may include low blood sugar, swollen abdomen due to an enlarged liver, and delayed growth.
